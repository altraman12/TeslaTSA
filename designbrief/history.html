Summary

In the summer of 1898, an inventor and scientist, Nikola Tesla, started it all by creating a small unmanned boat that could change direction based from verbal commands. This creation was the first utilization of radio frequency to control an object remotely. His research was continued by the works of Walter Righter and Richard Denny in the 1940s, with their creation of remote controlled drones. About 15,000 of Denny's drones were purchased by the military to train soldiers and do surveilence. Since then, drons have been expanded to consumer and corporate markets.

In 2010, drones came into public view as a device for aerial photography and video capture. The Parrot AR drone ignited the consumer drone industry, and ever since, many companies have cut into the market. These new personal drones have come to be known as "quadcopaters" due to utilizing four rotors to stabilize balance.

Three years ago, Amazon CEO, Jeff Bezos, announced the Amazon Prime Air initiative which would utilize drones in the shipment of Amazon orders weiging less than 5 lbs., consisting of about 89% of their sales.
