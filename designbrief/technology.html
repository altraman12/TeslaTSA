Hololens

Microsoft's Hololens is one of the most recent strides commerciall augmmented reality technology. Unlike its predecessor, the Google Glass, Hololens uses translucent OLED display to project information onto the user's surroundings. When combined with the 120 degree field-of-view camera array mounted on the front of the glasses, the headset is able to display 3 dimensional images that seem attached to real world objects.

Google Tango

Tango is an new initiative by Google to bring auugmented reality to the smartphone front. By working with smartphone manufacturers to embed more sensors in upcoming phones, Google is createing an ecosystem ripe for augmented reality applications. Sitting on top of those sensors is a collection of SDK (SOftware Development Kits) that allow developers readieer access to the new hardware.

Meta

In development since 2013, the Meta company's space glasses promise radical improvements to existing headsets. The glasses are built with a reflective surface that widens their field of view, potentially solving one of the industry's biggest concerns. Having launched its first developer kit in 2014, Meta is currently working on the second generation of the revolutionary product.
