Consumer

One popular application of augmented reality technlogy is navigation systems. In the near future, our GPS systems will be able to project our route on the road in front of us. There is also a lot of potential for AR technology in the tourism industry. Sightseeing locations can be sent to the user instead of the other way around.

Military

The United States Department of Defense has invested heavily in simulation and training technology for its troops. In addition, The battlefield presents numerous use cases for a heads up display. Fighter pilots, in particular, have to monitor several different variable. A system that could make this inforation more accessible could potentially be life saving.

Medical

The Madical industry is currently in need of methods to display vast amounts of information. Their vast use of imaging technologies requires doctors to be able to read and interpret a variety of scans. A virtual reality viewing system could make this task infinitely easier and more accurate.

Gaming

Augented reality gaming is already here. With Niantic's hit game, Pokemon Go, sweeping the nation, millions of smartphone users have already found themselves immersed in virtual realities. That being said, Pokemon go was neither the first nor the last attempt to apply Augmented reality to create immersive experiences. It only allowed for limited virtual interactions, and future attempts are sure to be more immersive.
