Commercial

With the ability to travel the skies rapidly, many ways to make money can be extracted from the use of drones.

One of the biggest proponents for drone use in a commercial setting is Amazon. Constantly Amazon strives to reduce delivery times and prices to attract more customers to the on-line shopping realm rather than "Brick and Mortar" stores. Currently due to high wage costs for delivery men and scheduled routes, Amazon is limited in both time and cost. By using drones as a delivery medium Amazon hopes to launch a program called Amazon Air. In this program drones will be used to deliver packages to your doorstep within 30 minutes. This would also be cheap for Amazon since they would only have to invest one time into drones rather than paying high wages. This would reduce prices for customers, making this form of delivery a viable option in the future.

Amazon faces many problems in developing this futuristic idea. Firstly weather is a big issue. Amazon cannot afford to be losing these drones to storms or gusts of wind, so the question remains how will the Amazon drones, which currently operate like regular drones, deal with these conditions. Next security is a big deal with this type of use. Drones being shot out of the sky to rob packages is a very big issues these drones face, since these drones are very fragile and will be impossible to monitor if shot. Finally a big problem Amazon air faces is figuring out the FAA regulations. If Amazon wants to progress with this scheme they will need to figure out a way to convince the public that constant drone hovering over people's heads is worth the faster shipping. All these difficulties and the difficulty of developing and manufacturing the technology makes this plan still years away.



Another commercial use of drones is the use of drones to deliver internet access rather than satellite. More publicly, Google has been working on Project Loon to create global internet access using balloons , but Mark Zuckerburg's Internet.orghopes to achieve the same goal using drones. These drones would be much cheaper to deploy and use in vast areas of undeveloped land and would work by acting as flying routers for people to connect to. This technology would help bring cheap internet access to millions in developing countries. These developments while seemingly philanthropic are also very commercial projects as well since they would allow these companies to pull in millions of new users to their websites.

Education

With fascinating new technology in drones they have a change to create a profound impact on our educational system.

One major projected use of drones, is drone building activities in schools. These activities teach kids nearly all of the major components of STEM, while giving them an enjoyable project worth striving for. Engineering, mathematics , physics , and computer science are all involved in the design of the drones, substantially increasing real world knowledge in these fields.


Drones can also educate young kids through exploration of nature around them using drones. By changing the perspective kids view the world drones can be used to cheaply teach very engaging lessons.

Even in college education this technology will be immensely useful with drone technology substantially increasing potential exploration projects. Drones can help immensely in projects ranging from ecosystem rehabilitation to nuclear waste observation.

As you might imagine drones can be an invaluable education tool for students of all ages.

Photography & Filming

Drones and quadcopters now typically come equipped with high definition cameras allowing filmakers to capture smooth aerial shots without requiring expensive jibs or helicopters. With adequate stabilization gear, drones can be effective tools for filming.

Agriculture

Farmers can use drones to monitor nitrogen levels, identify where resources are needed, and spot damaged areas. Drones have been programmed for precision farming, taking specific routes to analyze the growth of plants.
